Research Data Sources Reference

Economic Data

  • Local Area Unemployment Statistics (LAUS)

    Provides highly detailed, monthly estimates of labor force, employment, and unemployment metrics across localized regional geographies. It is frequently utilized to evaluate regional economic health and isolate localized labor market supply shocks.

    Bureau of Labor Statistics
  • Small Area Income and Poverty Estimates (SAIPE)

    Offers annual, localized estimates of low-income populations and median household income levels across counties and school districts. This dataset is standard for establishing baseline poverty thresholds for municipal socioeconomic evaluations.

    U.S. Census Bureau
  • American Community Survey (ACS)

    A foundational, continuous nationwide survey providing comprehensive longitudinal data on educational attainment, demographic shifts, housing characteristics, and detailed occupational frameworks. It serves as the primary benchmark for institutional baseline controls.

    U.S. Census Bureau
  • Current Population Survey (CPS)

    The primary source for monthly labor force characteristics and national employment data distributions. Its highly structural demographic supplemental components allow for rigorous evaluations of workforce participation dynamics.

    U.S. Census Bureau / BLS
  • IRS Individual Income Tax Statistics - ZIP Code Data

    Provides aggregate statistics on adjusted gross income, salary distributions, and filing characteristics mapped explicitly to individual 5-digit ZIP codes. This represents an exceptionally clean administrative baseline for localized household wealth tracking.

    Internal Revenue Service
  • Center for Microeconomic Data (Spending and Debt)

    Maintains the continuous consumer credit panel detailing longitudinal per-capita balances and delinquency rates across student loans, auto lines, and credit cards. It is heavily utilized to capture household credit health and regional debt burdens.

    Federal Reserve Bank of New York
  • Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF)

    A multi-year cross-sectional assessment of American household balance sheets tracking asset classes, pension values, liabilities, and net worth distributions. It functions as the premier source for wealth inequality evaluations.

    Federal Reserve Board
  • Survey of Income and Program Participation (SIPP)

    A continuous longitudinal panel designed to capture microeconomic shifts in household dynamics, cash transfers, and safety-net usage patterns over time. It provides high precision for tracking changes in public program engagement and economic stability.

    U.S. Census Bureau
  • Opportunity Insights Data Platform

    Provides accessible, localized metrics tracking economic mobility, intergenerational income transitions, and real-time consumption shifts across economic cohorts. This infrastructure is vital for assessing community-level upward mobility trajectories.

    Harvard University
  • UKCPR National Welfare Data

    A comprehensive state-level longitudinal dataset compiling public assistance structures, unemployment benefits, poverty baselines, and safety-net policy indicators. It provides researchers a unified framework for policy analysis.

    University of Kentucky Center for Poverty Research

Business Data

  • County Business Patterns (CBP)

    Tracks establishment counts, total first-quarter payroll parameters, and industry sizing across counties and ZIP codes.

    U.S. Census Bureau
  • Business Dynamics Statistics (BDS)

    Compiles net job creation, business lifespans, and firm startup patterns across precise regional dimensions.

    U.S. Census Bureau
  • Nonemployer Statistics

    Quantifies business activities and revenues for entities operating without official paid employees.

    U.S. Census Bureau

Housing Data

  • Zillow Research Data

    Maintains aggregate real estate performance values and dynamic market adjustments at diverse geographic levels.

    Zillow
  • Realtor.com Research Data

    Tracks real estate listing patterns, market velocities, and regional inventory profiles.

    Realtor.com

Health Data

  • CDC WONDER Public Databases

    An integrated platform tracking detailed public health reports, clinical parameters, and specific mortality tracking elements.

    CDC
  • BRFSS Surveillance Data

    Tracks behavioral risk factors, preventative medical selections, and chronic clinical dynamics over long timelines.

    CDC
  • Small Area Health Insurance Estimates (SAHIE)

    Offers model-based estimates of health insurance coverage statuses across localized county levels.

    U.S. Census Bureau

Crime & Policy Data

  • The National Neighborhood Data Archive (NaNDA)

    Tracks localized socio-environmental measures, resource distributions, and contextual indicators across neighbor networks.

    University of Michigan (ISR)
  • General Social Survey (GSS)

    Tracks long-term behavioral changes, social trends, and public outlook metrics within the demographic structure.

    NORC at University of Chicago
  • Arkansas Crime Statistics

    Maintains baseline operational crime registries and tracking profiles within state boundaries.

    AR Dept of Public Safety
